# Design Weight Cut Protocol

Plan a safe and effective weight reduction and rehydration strategy for competition weigh-in, distinguishing chronic body fat reduction from acute water manipulation, and maximizing performance restoration before competing.

## Why This Is Best Practice

**Adopted by:** USA Wrestling, USA Boxing, UFC Performance Institute, and elite combat sport national programs all provide structured weight management guidance. FINA, World Rowing Federation, and IJF have implemented weight monitoring protocols to prevent dangerous acute cuts. Most major combat sport organizations have moved to same-day or next-day weigh-ins to reduce the incentive for extreme acute cuts.
**Impact:** Reale et al. (2017) showed that rapid weight loss of >3% body mass in 24 hours significantly impairs strength, power, and reaction time that is not fully restored by competition time. Artioli et al. (2010) found that 91% of elite combat sport athletes engage in weight cutting, with 32% cutting >5% of body mass — at those magnitudes, performance impairment is severe and health risk is significant. A moderate cut (<3% body mass) with 24+ hours recovery allows near-complete rehydration and restoration of physical capacity.

## Steps

### 1. Classify the cut type needed

Distinguish two types of weight management:

**Chronic (gradual) weight loss (weeks to months):**
- Reduce body fat through sustainable caloric deficit (−250 to −500 kcal/day)
- Preserve lean mass with high protein intake (2.0-2.4 g/kg/day)
- Target race weight 4-8 weeks before competition (not the night before)
- Appropriate for most athletes needing to compete in a lower weight class long-term

**Acute (rapid) water weight manipulation (24-72 hours):**
- For small, short-duration cuts only (<3% body mass)
- Methods: reduce dietary fiber and residue (reduce gut content weight by 1-2kg), moderate sodium restriction, moderate fluid restriction, sweat via exercise or passive heat
- Not appropriate for cuts >5% body mass in 24 hours

### 2. Calculate the maximum safe acute cut

**Safe acute cut:** ≤3% of competition body mass with ≥24 hours recovery to weigh-in
- For a 70kg athlete: ≤2.1 kg water manipulation
- 3-5% with 24 hours: significant impairment risk; requires aggressive rehydration
- >5% in 24 hours: dangerous; performance severely impaired regardless of rehydration; associated with deaths in wrestling and judo

If the required cut exceeds 5% in 24 hours: the athlete is in the wrong weight class. Do not attempt.

### 3. Plan the weight reduction sequence (72 hours out)

**72 hours before weigh-in:**
- Begin low-residue diet (white rice, lean protein, limited vegetables) to reduce gut content
- Reduce dietary sodium to decrease water retention
- Maintain training but reduce volume (preserve performance)

**24-48 hours before:**
- Moderate fluid restriction (reduce to 1.5-2L/day from normal 3-4L)
- Optional: passive heat (sauna, hot bath) for last 0.5-1.0 kg if still above target
- No aggressive sweating methods unless weigh-in is ≥24 hours away

**Final hours:**
- Light exercise sweat session if needed for last adjustment
- Confirm body weight 3 hours before weigh-in

### 4. Design the rehydration and refueling plan (weigh-in to competition)

With 24+ hours recovery:
- Consume 150% of estimated fluid deficit over 4-6 hours
- Include sodium (1g/L in fluids or salty foods) to retain consumed fluid
- 1-4 g/kg carbohydrate within 4 hours post-weigh-in to restore glycogen
- Protein: 25-40g in the post-weigh-in meal for recovery signaling

With <2 hours recovery (same-day weigh-in):
- 10-15 mL/kg fluid in the first 30-60 min (small volume, frequent sips)
- High-GI carbohydrate: 60-75g within the first hour (gels, sports drink, white bread + jam)
- Protein: 20-25g; avoid high-fat foods (delay gastric emptying)
- Caffeine if used regularly: take 45-60 min before competition

### 5. Monitor safety indicators

Red flags requiring medical attention:
- Dizziness, syncope (fainting), or confusion during cutting
- Urine output stops or becomes brown (severe dehydration/rhabdomyolysis risk)
- Heart rate >100 bpm at rest during the cut
- Athlete reports numbness, cramps, or visual disturbance

## Common Mistakes

- **Extreme cuts in 24 hours:** The performance cost of >5% acute cuts far outweighs any competitive class advantage. Athletes who cut 10kg overnight regularly lose to smaller opponents at healthy weight.
- **Sweat suits and diuretics:** Diuretics are banned (WADA). Sauna suits increase dehydration rate but also increase heat illness risk.
- **No rehydration plan:** Athletes who cut weight without a structured recovery protocol consistently underperform.

## When NOT to Use

- Youth athletes: acute weight cutting is banned in many youth wrestling organizations and is contraindicated developmentally.
- Athletes with eating disorder history — any restrictive or manipulative nutrition protocol requires clinical oversight.
