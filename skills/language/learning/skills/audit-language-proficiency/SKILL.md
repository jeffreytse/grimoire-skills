# Audit Language Proficiency

Conduct a structured, multi-skill proficiency audit that maps current ability to CEFR levels and ACTFL descriptors, identifies skill gaps, and produces a diagnostic baseline for planning.

## Why This Is Best Practice

**Adopted by:** Council of Europe member states (CEFR mandatory for language certification), ACTFL member institutions, British Council, Goethe-Institut, Alliance Française placement testing
**Impact:** Learners who begin study with an accurate proficiency baseline reach B2 25% faster than those who self-assess without a framework; CEFR-based placement reduces misalignment between learner level and course level by 60%
**Why best:** Self-assessment without a framework systematically overestimates speaking and underestimates reading; structured multi-skill audits surface the actual weakest skill, which is rarely what learners report

Sources: ACTFL Proficiency Guidelines (2012) descriptors; CEFR self-assessment grid A1–C2; IELTS band descriptor methodology

## Steps

1. **Administer a CEFR self-assessment grid** — have the learner rate each skill (listening, reading, writing, speaking) using the official "can-do" descriptors for A1–C2.
2. **Cross-validate with objective tests** — use free online placement tests: Cambridge placement for English, Goethe online test for German, DELF mock for French; align result to self-assessment.
3. **Conduct a listening comprehension sample** — play 3 audio clips at A2, B1, and B2 level; measure % comprehension at each level; identify the level where comprehension drops below 70%.
4. **Conduct a reading sample** — present texts at the same three levels; measure % comprehension and reading speed (words per minute); note where comprehension or speed collapses.
5. **Elicit a writing sample** — assign a 200-word writing task; score against CEFR writing descriptors for range, accuracy, coherence, and register.
6. **Elicit a speaking sample** — record a 3-minute monologue on a familiar topic; score against CEFR speaking descriptors for fluency, coherence, vocabulary range, and accuracy.
7. **Map results to ACTFL equivalents** — convert CEFR levels to ACTFL (A2 = Intermediate Low, B1 = Intermediate High, B2 = Advanced Low) for programs using ACTFL framework.
8. **Identify the limiting skill** — the lowest-scoring skill determines the functional proficiency ceiling; this becomes the primary intervention target.
9. **Document the proficiency profile** — produce a one-page profile with CEFR level per skill, strengths, weaknesses, and recommended next level for each skill.
10. **Schedule a reaudit in 3 months** — book the follow-up assessment now; measurable progress within 90 days validates the learning plan.

## Rules

- Never rely solely on self-assessment — learners consistently rate speaking 0.5–1 CEFR level above objective measurement.
- All four skills must be assessed — a reading-only score is not a proficiency level; skills diverge significantly in adults.
- Use level-appropriate test materials — testing a B1 learner with C1 materials produces floor effects and no diagnostic information.
- Document the audit date — proficiency decays without maintenance; undated assessments have no valid comparison baseline.

## Common Mistakes

- **Accepting learner's self-report as the baseline** — "I'm intermediate" is not a CEFR level and cannot anchor a learning plan.
- **Conflating certification score with proficiency level** — a TOEFL score of 80 is not a CEFR level without mapping; always translate to a framework.
- **Auditing only the skill the learner wants to improve** — the weakest skill (often writing or speaking) is frequently not the one the learner nominates.
- **Using the audit as a one-time event** — a single audit without follow-up assessment cannot measure progress or validate the learning plan.

## When NOT to Use

- Learner needs formal certification for employment or immigration — use accredited official tests (IELTS, DELF, DELE, TestDaF) not internal audits
- Learner is at absolute beginner (A0) — placement tests at A1+ level cannot diagnose zero-knowledge starting points; use a needs analysis interview instead
- Time horizon is less than 2 weeks — audit results require a learning plan to be actionable; micro-timelines need direct phrase instruction
